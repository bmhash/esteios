# Chapters — Writing Instructions

Each chapter in `chapters/` follows a specific structure and belongs to one of the three circles.

## Chapter Structure

Every chapter must include:
1. **Title** in Portuguese (frontmatter)
2. **Epigraph** — Stoic or Greek philosophical quote
3. **Concept box** — Greek term with Portuguese definition
4. **Prose** — 1-4 pages, second person address to daughter

## Writing Guidelines

- **Chapters are 1-4 pages** — short, focused, digestible
- **Second person** — addressing the daughter directly (tu)
- **Voice**: Direct, honest, unpretentious — like Marcus Aurelius
- **No performance** — no showing off, no literary flourishes
- **Simple language** with philosophical depth
- **European Portuguese (PT-PT)** with old orthography

## AI Role When Working in Chapters

- **NEVER generate prose** — the author writes all content
- **NEVER rewrite** unless explicitly asked
- Help find epigraphs using `/extract-quote`
- Verify Greek terms and transliterations
- Check chapter structure when asked
- Provide word count when requested
- Suggest cross-references to other chapters
- Be terse when the author is writing — don't interrupt flow

## Three Circles Organization

- **Círculo I — Logos** (O Eu Interior): Chapters 1, 2, 4, 10
- **Círculo II — Ethos** (Com os Outros): Chapters 3, 5, 6, 7, 11, 12, 13
- **Círculo III — Cosmos** (No Mundo): Chapters 8, 9, 14, 15

Each chapter should align with its circle's theme.
