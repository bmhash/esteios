# Stoic Philosophy Research

## Core Stoic Concepts

### The Three Disciplines (Epictetus)
1. **Discipline of Assent** (logos) — judgment, perception, what we believe
2. **Discipline of Action** (ethos) — how we act toward others, social virtue
3. **Discipline of Desire** (cosmos) — what we want, our relationship with the universe

These map to the three circles of Esteios:
- Logos → Círculo I (O Eu Interior)
- Ethos → Círculo II (Com os Outros)
- Cosmos → Círculo III (No Mundo)

### The Four Cardinal Virtues
1. **Wisdom** (phronesis / φρόνησις) — practical wisdom, good judgment
2. **Courage** (andreia / ἀνδρεία) — fortitude, facing fear
3. **Justice** (dikaiosyne / δικαιοσύνη) — fairness, treating others rightly
4. **Temperance** (sophrosyne / σωφροσύνη) — moderation, self-control

### Key Stoic Terms

**Greek → Portuguese**
- **arete** (ἀρετή) — virtude, excelência
- **apatheia** (ἀπάθεια) — serenidade, ausência de paixões perturbadoras
- **ataraxia** (ἀταραξία) — tranquilidade, paz de espírito
- **eudaimonia** (εὐδαιμονία) — florescimento, vida boa
- **prohairesis** (προαίρεσις) — escolha moral, vontade
- **oikeiosis** (οἰκείωσις) — apropriação, identificação com o todo
- **sympatheia** (συμπάθεια) — interconexão, simpatia cósmica
- **katalepsis** (κατάληψις) — compreensão, apreensão cognitiva
- **askesis** (ἄσκησις) — prática, exercício espiritual

## Stoic Practices

### Negative Visualization (premeditatio malorum)
Imagining loss to appreciate what you have and prepare for adversity.

### View from Above (cosmological perspective)
Seeing yourself from a distance to gain perspective on your place in the cosmos.

### Morning and Evening Reflection
Marcus Aurelius's practice of reviewing the day and preparing for tomorrow.

### Dichotomy of Control
Distinguishing what is in your power (judgments, actions) from what is not (external events).

## Key Source Texts

### Marcus Aurelius — Meditations
- Private journal, never meant for publication
- 12 books of reflections
- Written in Greek while on military campaign
- Central themes: acceptance, mortality, duty, virtue
- Available in: `data/Marcus Aurelius - The Meditations of Marcus Aurelius.pdf` (English)

### Epictetus — Enchiridion
- "Handbook" of Stoic practice
- Compiled by student Arrian
- Practical guidance for daily life
- Opening: "Some things are in our control, others are not"

### Werner Jaeger — Paideia
- Study of Greek education and culture
- Three volumes on the formation of Greek character
- Connects philosophy to education (paideia)
- Available in: `data/Werner Jaeger-Paideia.pdf` (Brazilian Portuguese)

## Paideia Concept

**Paideia** (παιδεία) — Greek concept of education as character formation, not just knowledge transfer.

Key aspects:
- Holistic formation of the person
- Virtue through education
- Cultural transmission across generations
- Excellence (arete) as the goal
- Community responsibility for education

This is the foundation of Esteios — transmitting wisdom from father to daughter.
